### Topic 1: Foundations of SNA vs. Traditional Analysis
**Q1: A dangerous rumor causes panic in a town despite having no central author, headquarters, or massive reach account.** Explain why traditional data analysis fails to find the root cause of this panic, and how Social Network Analysis (SNA) reframes the investigation. *(2 marks)*

**Q2: Two employees in a corporation possess identical qualifications and resources, yet one has significantly higher influence over company decisions.** Using the intellectual foundation of SNA, explain how this disparity occurs. *(2 marks)*

**Q3: In a Knowledge Graph representing a cybersecurity curriculum, if "Phishing" and "Malware" are nodes, what do the edges represent?** How does interpreting this graph fundamentally differ from analyzing a social network? *(2 marks)*

### Topic 2: Network Topologies (Scale-Free & Small-World Structures)
**Q1: A social media platform decides to randomly ban 5,000 user accounts to curb a misinformation campaign, but the network's overall information flow remains highly active.** Explain this operational failure using the properties of a "Scale-Free Structure." *(2 marks)*

**Q2: How does the "Small-World Effect" (SWE) mathematically explain the rapid mobilization of sudden political protests?** Refer to its two key metrics in your answer. *(2 marks)*

**Q3: When examining a growing network, an analyst notes that an already popular influencer is gaining followers at a much faster rate than a new user.** Identify this phenomenon and explain how it contributes to the vulnerability of the network. *(2 marks)*

### Topic 3: Centrality Metrics & Strategic Disruption
**Q1: In a covert terror network, a courier is found to have the highest degree centrality, while the main strategist has a degree of only 2.** Why does degree centrality fail as a measure of true power in this scenario? *(2 marks)*

**Q2: You are tasked with disrupting a coordination network by removing two nodes: Node A (highest degree) and Node C (highly trusted leader of a specific cluster).** Why is this considered a "structurally naive" strategy? *(2 marks)*

**Q3: An analyst relies strictly on Betweenness Centrality to find the gatekeeper of information in a corporate hierarchy.** Describe a real-world organizational behavior that would render this mathematical metric operationally irrelevant. *(2 marks)*

### Topic 4: Information Diffusion Models
**Q1: Why would the SI (Susceptible-Infected) model be mathematically inappropriate for predicting the lifespan of exam gossip on campus?** Which model should be used instead? *(2 marks)*

**Q2: A new controversial privacy policy is introduced on a messaging app. Users only switch to a rival app if 60% of their contacts have already switched.** Which diffusion model applies here, and why does it result in a "cascade or nothing" curve rather than a smooth bell curve? *(2 marks)*

**Q3: In the SIR model, if the speed of forgetting ($\gamma$) is significantly faster than the speed of spread ($\beta$), what happens to the reproduction number ($R_0$) and the resulting diffusion?** *(2 marks)*

### Topic 5: Echo Chambers, Polarization, & Interventions
