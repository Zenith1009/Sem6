### 1. Classification of Entrepreneur (Guaranteed Question)
**The Question:** *"What type of entrepreneur are you guys, and what type of enterprise is CampusRun?"*

**How to Answer (Use these exact keywords):**
Tell the panel that the CampusRun team represents a mix of specific entrepreneurial traits taught in Unit 1:
*   **By Technology:** We are **Technical Entrepreneurs** because our core product relies on a tech-stack (WhatsApp API, Progressive Web App, Native Android App) to solve a logistical problem. 
*   **By Adaptation:** We are **Adaptive/Imitating Entrepreneurs**. We did not invent hyperlocal delivery (like Swiggy/Dunzo), but we are adapting their successful innovations specifically for the under-served micro-environment of the SVNIT campus.
*   **By Motivation:** We are **Spontaneous Entrepreneurs**, because we noticed a natural, unaddressed gap (students wasting 20-45 minutes on errands) and acted quickly on the idea.
*   **By Stage:** We are **First-generation Entrepreneurs**, as we are building this without inheriting a family business.

**Pro-Tip for the Exam/Viva:** 
> *Memory Trick:* Think of the phrase **"Tech Adapts Spontaneously First"** to remember your 4 classifications (Technical, Adaptive, Spontaneous, First-generation). 

### 2. Marketing Management: STP & 4Ps (High Weightage Topic)
**The Question:** *"Explain your target audience and marketing strategy. How will you position this?"*

**How to Answer:**
Do not just say "we will market to students." Use the **STP Framework** (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning):
*   **Segmentation:** We segmented the market **Geographically** (SVNIT Campus - 1.5 km radius) and **Demographically/Behaviorally** (Time-constrained B.Tech/M.Tech students living in 12 hostels).
*   **Targeting:** We are using a **Single Segment Concentrated Strategy**. This means we are offering one specific service (peer-to-peer delivery) to one specific group (SVNIT hostel residents).
*   **Positioning:** Our brand position is built on **"Trust Through Verification."** Unlike commercial apps that stop at the gate, our positioning is a safe, student-only ecosystem.

Next, explain your **Marketing Mix (4Ps)**:
*   **Product:** A hyperlocal errand marketplace with zero-infrastructure.
*   **Price:** ₹25 per task (Affordable value pricing) and a ₹99/month CampusRun Pass (Subscription model).
*   **Place:** The 12 on-campus hostels of SVNIT.
*   **Promotion:** WhatsApp seeding, Hostel Ambassadors, and physical poster campaigns on notice boards.

### 3. The New Product Development (NPD) Process (Crucial Concept)
**The Question:** *"How did you go about developing CampusRun from an idea to a product?"*

**How to Answer:**
Map your Go-to-Market (GTM) and Tech Roadmap directly to the **8 Steps of the NPD Process**:
1.  **Idea Generation:** Noticing the friction of tightly packed engineering schedules and delayed parcels.
2.  **Idea Screening:** Checking feasibility—realizing commercial apps (Dunzo/Blinkit) don't work inside campuses, but students have UPI and phones.
3.  **Concept Development & Testing:** This is your **Stealth Phase**. You tested the core concept with 10 hand-picked partners and 20-30 test users via manual WhatsApp orders to validate operations.
4.  **Marketing Strategy:** Designing the referral programs, ₹200-300 ambassador credits, and poster campaigns.
5.  **Business Analysis:** Calculating your Unit Economics (₹25 fee, 20% platform commission, 1.5% gateway fee = ₹4.62 net revenue per task) to ensure profitability.
6.  **Product Development:** Building the actual tech in phases (Phase 1: WhatsApp API, Phase 2: PWA, Phase 3: Android App).
7.  **Market Testing:** Your **Soft Launch**. Releasing only in H1-H4 hostels to gather completion data and fix bugs.
8.  **Commercialization:** Your **Full Launch**. Expanding to all SVNIT hostels and buildings with full promotional backing.

### 4. Motivation & Entrepreneurial Models
**The Question:** *"What is driving your team to build this? Why not just study and take placements?"*

**How to Answer:**
Speak in the language of **David McClelland’s Theory of Achievement Motivation**. 
*   State that your team is driven by the **Need for Achievement (nAch)**. You have a strong drive to excel, solve the specific problem of "informal broken WhatsApp groups," and create value from scratch.
*   Mention that you possess an **Internal Locus of Control** (Rotter's Theory). You believe that the success of CampusRun depends on your team's planning (your phased tech roadmap and zero-infrastructure model) rather than external luck.

### 5. Risk, Threats, and "Industrial Sickness"
**The Question:** *"What are the risks to your business, and why might it fail?"*

**How to Answer:**
The professors love to test your knowledge of **Business Environment (PESTLE)** and **Industrial Sickness**.
Frame your SWOT Analysis "Threats" using syllabus terminology:
*   **Administrative Management Risks:** Founder dropout due to academic pressure or graduation timelines.
*   **Legal/Political Environment (PESTLE):** Administration friction or potential regulatory concerns from SVNIT authorities regarding students doing commercial deliveries.
*   **Market Constraints:** The single-campus limitation (SVNIT) caps revenue. To prevent "sickness" (continuous losses), you have a mitigation strategy: **National Expansion** to 31 NITs and 3,500+ engineering colleges.

### 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid in the Presentation:
1.  **Ignoring the "Competitor" slide:** When they ask about Swiggy/Zomato, clearly use the word **"Market Position"**. Say, *"We operate in an uncontested niche because Swiggy has geographical limitations (gate delivery only), creating an Opportunity for us."*.
2.  **Talking only about tech:** You are CSE students, so you will naturally focus on the "PWA and API". *Do not do this.* The evaluators are management professors. Focus heavily on the **Unit Economics slide** (the ₹4.62 net revenue and Operating Leverage). Explain how fixed costs spread as volume grows.
3.  **Using casual words for marketing:** Instead of saying "We will tell our friends in hostels," say "We are executing a **Selective Distribution strategy** utilizing **Hostel Ambassadors** for grassroots promotion".

**Final advice from a senior:** Speak confidently about your numbers (15% adoption = 750 users). If you map your "Soft Launch" to "Market Testing" and your "Unit Economics" to "Business Analysis," you will easily score maximum marks. Good luck with CampusRun!
